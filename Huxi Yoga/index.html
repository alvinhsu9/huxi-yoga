About Us

Hūxī, in Mandarin means:
To Breathe

It's been a tough 2 years for not just you, but for the world. Here at Hūxī we have one purpose, helping everyone find their inner peace and to silence the outside noise.

After struggling with anxiety for many years, CEO Ryan Topp looked into different methods to help him cope. With the stress of the COVID pandemic and trying to maintain balance with his work/social life, he could feel his mental health deteriorating.

After many trial runs at different coping methods, he stumbled upon yoga. Not only did it help calm him mentally, but it helped with his physical health as well. After years of stretching, posing, and breathing techniques, he finally felt that he had found his inner peace.

Hoping to share the knowledge and wisdom he has picked up along the way, he created Hūxī. A yoga meditation studio with the goal of helping all achieve mental stability.

Other methods

Social connection can lower anxiety and depression, help us regulate our emotions, lead to higher self-esteem and empathy, and actually improve our immune systems. By neglecting our need to connect, we put our health at risk.

Exercise improves mental health by reducing anxiety, depression, and negative mood and by improving self-esteem and cognitive function. Exercise has also been found to alleviate symptoms such as low self-esteem and social withdrawal.

Studies have shown that simply talking about our problems and sharing our negative emotions with someone we trust can be profoundly healing—reducing stress, strengthening our immune system, and reducing physical and emotional distress.

The mental health benefits of meditation include better focus and concentration, improved self-awareness and self-esteem, lower levels of stress and anxiety, and fostering kindness. Meditation also has benefits for your physical health, as it can improve your tolerance for pain and help fight substance addiction.

A healthy, well-balanced diet can help us think clearly and feel more alert. It can also improve concentration and attention span. Conversely, an inadequate diet can lead to fatigue, impaired decision-making, and can slow down reaction time.

Sufficient sleep, especially REM sleep, facilitates the brain's processing of emotional information. During sleep, the brain works to evaluate and remember thoughts and memories, and it appears that a lack of sleep is especially harmful to the consolidation of positive emotional content.

Spending time on an activity that you enjoy can improve your mental health and wellbeing. Research shows that people with hobbies are less likely to suffer from stress, low mood, and depression. Activities that get you out and about can make you feel happier and more relaxed.
